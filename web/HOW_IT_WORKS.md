# The demo site — how every tab works

A tabbed, animated walkthrough of the whole project. Every number comes from
`../data.json`, regenerated by `../export_web_data.py` from the verified
`run_*.py` outputs. Charts: Chart.js. Animations: GSAP. 3D: Three.js. Swarm +
algorithm visuals: custom HTML5 canvas / SVG. No build step — pure static files.

---

## 1 · Overview
**Shows:** the one-sentence spine, three headline numbers (58 checks · 40% fresher
· +9% energy — animated count-ups), a **3D swarm** you can drag to orbit (Three.js:
teal = drones, violet = fast-decaying targets, lines = comm links), and a card
grid that jumps to each tab.
**Why it matters:** sets the objective the professor asked for and lets a reviewer
navigate the evidence in one click.

## 2 · Swarm (live simulation)
**Shows:** N drones each patrolling a disk and re-anchoring on a moving target;
comm links appear between drones in range; a dashed line is the sensing beam
(bright when the drone is locked on).
**Controls & significance:**
- **Target motion** (static / dynamic / maneuvering) — switches the motion model
  (`wan/targets.py`): static = the paper's assumption; dynamic = constant velocity;
  maneuvering = coordinated turns. Watch the targets get harder to follow.
- **Drones** — swarm size N; rebuilds the scene.
- **Speed** — target speed (agility); faster targets drift further each round.
- **Pause / reset** — freeze or regenerate.
**Read it as:** the scenario the whole paper operates on — and why a *moving*
target is the gap the base paper ignores.

## 3 · H-MAP (hierarchical aggregation)
**Shows:** the knockout tournament as a left-to-right bracket. Column "agents" →
"round 1" → "round 2" → "root". Each node has a **payload ring**; on a merge the
sender's ring shrinks (compression η), a glowing packet flies to the partner, the
ring grows (fusion), and the sender fades (retires). It **replays the real pairing
trace** from the engine.
**Controls & significance:**
- **Paper / Ours** — Paper compresses maximally, so the **report-quality meter**
  (D vs the fidelity floor) ends *over the floor* (red). Ours keeps compression
  interior + freshness-aware, so it stays *under the floor* (green).
- **Play / step / reset** — auto-run, single merge, or restart.
- **Cumulative-energy meter** fills to the real mission total; **quality meter**
  shows the distortion D against D_max.
**Read it as:** the core mechanism the paper proposes — and the exact place our
fidelity + freshness changes bite.

## 4 · Blossom (max-weight matching)
**Shows:** one round's agents on a ring with candidate-pair **edge weights**
(energies). "Optimal (Blossom)" highlights the minimum-total-weight perfect
matching; greedy and random highlight worse matchings; the table tallies total
energy.
**Controls & significance:**
- **New instance** — fresh random weights.
- **Optimal / greedy / random** — compare the exact matcher to the baselines.
**Read it as:** the per-round pairing is solved *exactly* in polynomial time —
which is why metaheuristics (next tab) can only tie it, never beat it.

## 5 · Learned · RL (predictive topology)
**Shows:** the ζ-fragility curve — the paper's hand-tuned knob makes energy
**wobble** (gray) while the learned value model V̂(state) is a **flat lower line**
(teal). Plus the optimality-gap bars (learned vs paper vs the ACO/ABC/Cuckoo/
Egyptian-Vulture baselines vs random).
**Controls & significance:**
- **ζ slider** — drag the paper's knob; the readout shows the paper's energy at
  that ζ vs the learned policy, which needs *no knob*.
**Read it as:** Direction 2 — replace a fragile hand-tuned heuristic with a
learned cost-to-go that lands 3.6% from optimal vs the paper's 14.7%.

## 6 · Freshness (the spine)
**Shows, top to bottom:**
- the **negative result** banner (40.7 m of chase demanded → 0 J spent: motion
  is inert through the mobility channel);
- **D1** — a dual-axis chart: faster target → η-floor rises 0.549→0.719 and energy
  +9%;
- **D2** — value-prioritized topology: 40% fresher at no energy cost;
- **spread** — the advantage grows 21%→48% with agility heterogeneity;
- **prediction** — CV-Kalman and CV/CT IMM beat the naive predictor per class.
**Read it as:** the whole contribution — target motion enters through information
freshness, driving compression (D1) and topology (D2).

## 7 · Graphs (figure gallery)
**Shows:** every figure the pipeline produces (20 of them), grouped by
contribution, each with a one-line "what it proves". Click any to enlarge
(lightbox).
**Read it as:** the raw evidence — each image is emitted by a `run_*.py` script.

## 8 · Verify
**Shows:** the 58-check breakdown by module, plus the energy–fidelity Pareto
frontier (paper's point dominated) and the washout chart (displacement vs patrol
radius).
**Read it as:** every headline claim is an automated PASS check that regenerates
from code in minutes.

## 9 · Before / After
**Shows:** animated paper-vs-ours bars for the four headline changes, then the
full comparison table across every dimension.
**Read it as:** the one-screen summary for a reviewer.

---

### Honesty notes
- Charts and headline numbers are **real engine output**. The swarm motion and the
  H-MAP payload-ring/quality animations are **illustrative of the mechanics**; the
  H-MAP *pairing trace* and the energy/quality endpoints are real.
- Regenerate everything: `python export_web_data.py` (after rerunning any
  `run_*.py`) refreshes `data.json`, and the gallery reads `figures/`.
